Forklift safety

Daily driver list: Pre-shift inspection form for electric and propane forklifts.

Inspection of the machinery is required prior to using it. This form must be completed one hour before the start of the shift (In accordance with the regulations established by OHSA).

1. Forks, backrest, locking pins, hoses, hydraulic controls:
  • Inspect forks for warping, cracks or excessive wear.
  • Check that the backrest is securely secured and undamaged.
  • Check that the locking pins are in place and working properly.
  • Examine hydraulic hoses for leaks, wear or damage.
  • Test hydraulic controls to ensure smooth, uninterrupted operation.

2. Tires, lug nuts, roof covering:
  • Evaluate tires for wear, cuts, and proper pressure.
  • Check that all lug nuts are tight and in good condition.
  • Inspect the roof covering for damage or insecurities.
{{ form.check2 }}
{{ form.des2 }}
3. Lights, horn, capacity plate, gauges, display panel:
  • Check the operation of all lights, including warning lights.
  • Test the horn to make sure it is audible and works properly.
  • Verify that the capacity plate is present, legible and up to date.
  • Check all gauges to ensure they show accurate readings.
  • Evaluate the display panel to confirm that all indications are clear and functional.
{{ form.check3 }}
{{ form.des3 }}
4. Energy source in optimal conditions:
  • Inspect the battery (for electric forklifts) to ensure it is clean, charged and undamaged.
  • In the case of fuel forklifts, check fuel levels and quality.
{{ form.check4 }}
{{ form.des4 }}
5. Seat sensor:
  • Check that the sensor in the seat works correctly.

{{ form.des5 }}
6. Tilt, Hoist, Sideshift Lift Functions:
  • Test the tilt ability to make sure it is smooth and consistent.
  • Check the lifting function to detect any irregularities or difficulties.
  • Check lateral displacement to ensure correct and fluid movement.

{{ form.des6 }}
7. Parking break:
  • Test the parking brake to ensure that it engages and releases properly.

8. Brakes:
  • Evaluate the brakes during movement to confirm their effectiveness and response.

9. Seat and safety belt:
  • Inspect the seat to ensure that it is in good condition and securely anchored.
  • Check the seat belt to ensure its correct operation and absence of wear.

10. Directional:
  • Test turn signals to confirm that they function properly and are visible.
{{ form.check10 }}
{{ form.des10 }}
11. Strobe light::
  • Check the strobe light to make sure it works and is visible from all directions.

{{ form.des11 }}
12. Fire extinguisher:
  • Check for the presence of the fire extinguisher, ensure it is within its expiration date and is accessible for immediate use.
